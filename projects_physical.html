Physical-Layer Network Coding

Physical-Layer Network Coding (PNC) revolves around the concept of deliberate interference. A group of users communicate via a relay, in the same time slot. This results in the received signal at the relay being a superposition of all signals sent by the users in that time slot. The two user case has been considered in extensive detail in literature, referred to as the Two Way Relay Channel (TWRC). Here we consider the TWRC and consider the most promising techniques in the multi-way relaying case where we have more than two users. Throughout we consider perfect synchronisation between user nodes. All other concepts will be introduced as required in the form of diagrams and animations, with mathematical formulations being provided for further detail.

The AWGN channel. In the presence of Additive White Gaussian Noise our decision rule becomes a maximum likelihood estimation, where we assume that each symbol from the sender's alphabet has the same a priori probability. This rule is therefore, "Assign $\hat{m} = m_k$ if my received signal is closer to $s_k$ than to any other signal vector".

We first consider the Two-Way Relay Channel (TWRC) as a foundation for the more involved multi-way relaying situation. We have two users, each able to send 4 distinct signals. One possible realisation of this is Quadrature Phase Shift Keying. We intentionally let the users communicate at the same time, allowing interference. The relay receives a signal of the following form: $$H_{1}x_{1} + H_{2}x_{2} + n_{R}$$

If we now allow each user to send $8$ distinct signals the relay can receive a total of $8^{2}$ or $64$ possible values. This can also be visualised by varying the fading coefficients for each of the two channels.
